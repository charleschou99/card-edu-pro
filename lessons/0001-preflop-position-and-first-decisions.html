Lesson 0001: Preflop Position and First Decisions

Estimated time: 15 minutes · Track: competition path (cash-first foundation)

Mission tie-in: Competition players win by repeatedly making small high-quality decisions. Your first edge is not fancy bluffs; it is position discipline and clean preflop choices.

What you will be able to do today

  • Identify where position sits in 6-max hand selection.
  • Make a basic open-or-fold preflop choice from position.
  • Estimate whether a call is too expensive using quick pot-odds thresholds.

Concept 1: Position is your first permanent edge

In 6-max, you generally act from early to late position in this order: UTG -> HJ -> CO -> BTN -> SB -> BB.

The later you act, the more information you have, so your opening range can be wider. Early position is disciplined and tight; button is widest.

Reference: position order cheatsheet

Concept 2: Starter preflop baseline

As a beginner in foundation phase, do this:

  • UTG/HJ: play tight, avoid marginal offsuit hands.
  • CO: widen moderately.
  • BTN: widest open range.
  • If uncertain: fold and note the spot for review.

Reference: RFI baseline table

Concept 3: Pot-odds guardrail (quick use)

If facing a half-pot bet, you need roughly 25% equity to call. If facing a pot-sized bet, you need about 33%.

When unsure, use this fast estimate: on the flop, outs x 4; on the turn, outs x 2.

Reference: pot-odds quick table

Interactive drill (retrieval practice)

Answer from memory first, then check feedback. This builds storage strength.

Drill A: UTG with K9o, unopened pot. Best default?
Drill B: BTN with A8s, unopened pot. Best default?

Today in your 30-minute play block

  1. Before every preflop action, say your position out loud.
  2. Use fold as default when unsure in early position.
  3. Tag 3 hands where you hesitated and save them for review.
